Matt Eva

Software Developer, Field Service Engineer, Writer, and Former Educator

Hi there! I'm Matt. I'm a multi-disciplinary professional with experience across industries, mediums, and subject matters.

Throughout my life, I've bridged the gap between "left brain" and "right brain" pursuits. I started my college journey as a Physics major, but graduated with an English degree - in contrast, I started my career as a content writer and editor, but have since taught software engineering and now work as a field service engineer in the semiconductor industry. Go figure!

If my career path has been anything, it's been "non-traditional". For example, between 2019 and 2021, I moved away from my home region - the San Francisco Bay Area - to Eugene, Oregon, where I spent my time working a variety of different jobs, meeting people from all walks of life, writing, and thinking a lot.

That time in my life doesn't really show up on a resume, but it was invaluable for my personal growth. The relative quiet of Oregon, and the refreshing newness of a different place, gave me the space I needed to reflect upon and examine my life, what mattered to me, and what I felt was truly worth pursuing.

Since then, I've migrated back to the Bay Area, and have continued to amass a broad spectrum of knowledge and work experience. After enrolling in a programming bootcamp with the goal of building my own platform, and subsequently teaching at that same bootcamp for a few years, I industry hopped again to the hardware side of the tech industry as a field service engineer. I've traveled all across the US, to two different countries (and counting), and a host of different customers. It feels great to get out from behind my computer for a change and play an integral role in the reliability sector of the entire global semiconductor manufacturing industry.

One theme has become clear, however - I thrive in spaces where I can educate, organize, and create, and take technical complexity and distill it into something legible and useful for audiences of every technical level. I'm drawn to any kind of role that allows me to balance my love of writing, teaching, and working with people with my love for logical thinking, system design, and creative problem solving.

If I seem like someone you'd be interested in connecting with, don't hesitate to reach out!
